## 1. The Bootstrapped Growth Framework

When you're bootstrapped, you don't have money. You have **equity in relationships, margin to share, and a product that sells itself once people see it.** Your strategy must be:

```
PHASE 1 (Month 1-3):   Hustle channels — YOU + Travis + friends selling directly
PHASE 2 (Month 3-6):   Partner channels — agencies, ambassadors, trade networks
PHASE 3 (Month 6-12):  Paid channels — reinvest revenue into ads + content
```

The force multiplier concept is correct. You need to **turn other people into your sales team** by making the economics irresistible for them. Here's how.

## 2. Channel 1: Referral Partners (Marketing Agencies & Dev Shops)

### Why This Is Your #1 Channel

There are ~50,000 local marketing agencies and web development shops in the US that serve small businesses. They already have:
- **Trust** with the business owner (the owner hired them)
- **Regular touchpoints** (monthly SEO reports, website updates)
- **Motivation** to add revenue streams (most agencies are 10-40% margin)
- **The exact customer you want** (local businesses who need digital presence help)

A marketing agency that builds websites for plumbers is literally sitting on your customer list.

### The Offer — "White-Label AI Receptionist"

**Don't approach agencies as a referral partner. Approach them as a reseller.**

```
"Hey [Agency], your clients are missing calls and losing money.
 We built an AI receptionist that answers calls 24/7, books appointments,
 and integrates with their CRM.

 You can resell it to your clients at YOUR markup.
 We charge you $199/mo wholesale. You charge them $349-499/mo.
 You keep the spread + 20% of usage revenue.
 
 You already talk to these businesses every month.
 This is an extra $150-300/mo per client for you, zero extra work."
```

### Commission Structure for Agencies

| Model | What They Get | Your Revenue | Their Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reseller (preferred)** | Wholesale pricing + usage share | $199/mo + 80% usage | $100-300/mo markup + 20% usage |
| **Referral** | 25% of base subscription, forever | 75% of base + 100% usage | 25% of base (~$75/mo on Growth) |
| **One-time bounty** | $500 per activated customer | Full revenue after bounty | $500 flat |

**Recommendation: Offer BOTH reseller and referral options.** Let the agency choose. Sophisticated agencies want to resell (higher margin). Smaller shops just want a referral check.

### Why NOT 30% Lifetime

You mentioned 30% lifetime. Here's the math:

- Growth plan at $299/mo: 30% = $89.70/mo to the partner, forever
- With usage (~$400/mo): 30% of $699 = $209.70/mo to the partner
- Over 2 years: $5,033 paid to the partner per customer

That's a LOT. But here's the thing — **if the alternative is $0 customers, then 70% of something beats 100% of nothing.**

**My recommendation:** 

- **25% of base subscription for 12 months** (not lifetime, not usage)
- After 12 months, it drops to **10% ongoing**
- Usage revenue is YOURS (this is where most of the money is)

This means on a Growth customer:
- Year 1: Partner gets $299 × 25% × 12 = **$897**
- Year 2+: Partner gets $299 × 10% × 12 = **$358/yr**
- You keep: ~$4,500/yr in base + ~$4,800/yr in usage = **$9,300/yr**
- Partner's cut is ~10% of total revenue — very sustainable

### How to Find Agencies

1. **Google "marketing agency for [plumbers/HVAC/electricians] [city]"** — they SEO for exactly this
2. **Clutch.co** — filter by industry (Home Services), location, size
3. **Local Chamber of Commerce directories** — agencies are members
4. **LinkedIn** — search "marketing agency owner" + "home services" or "contractor"
5. **Thumbtack/Yelp** — search "marketing consultant" in target cities

**Target: 20 agency conversations in Month 1. If 3-5 sign up as partners, each bringing 2-3 clients in the first quarter = 6-15 new customers from this channel alone.**

## 3. Channel 2: Trade Network Recruiting (Travis & Blue Collar)

### The Travis Play

Travis is pure gold. Here's why:
- He's IN the trades. He has credibility that you don't.
- Electricians talk to plumbers, who talk to HVAC guys. It's a tight community.
- Blue collar business owners trust word-of-mouth from peers MORE than any ad.

### The Travis Deal

**Don't give Travis 50% forever. Here's a smarter structure:**

| Element | Deal |
|---|---|
| **First 10 customers Travis brings** | 40% of base subscription for 12 months |
| **Customers 11-50** | 30% of base for 12 months |
| **After 12 months** | Drops to 10% ongoing |
| **Usage revenue** | 0% (you keep all usage) |
| **One-time bonus** | $200 per activated customer (paid immediately) |

On a Growth customer ($299/mo):
- Travis gets $200 cash day 1 + $120/mo for 12 months = **$1,640 per customer in Year 1**
- If Travis brings 10 customers: **$16,400 in Year 1**
- That's real money for a blue collar guy, and it costs you ~18% of total revenue (including usage)

**The pitch to Travis:**
```
"Travis, every electrician you know is missing calls and losing jobs.
 I built an AI that answers their phone 24/7 and books appointments.
 
 For every electrician, plumber, or HVAC guy you introduce me to who signs up,
 I'll pay you $200 cash the day they activate, plus $120/month for a year.
 
 You bring me 10 guys, that's $2,000 cash + $1,200/month.
 That's $16,400 in your pocket this year for making introductions.
 
 All you gotta do is say: 'Hey man, you gotta check out this thing 
 that answers your phone when you're on a job. It's insane.'"
```

### Expanding Travis's Network

Once Travis brings his immediate contacts:
1. Ask Travis to introduce you to **his suppliers** (electrical supply houses, plumbing supply stores). These guys see EVERY contractor in town.
2. Ask if he attends any **trade association meetings** (NECA, PHCC, local contractor meetups)
3. **Offer Travis a "team" structure** — if he recruits OTHER blue collar guys to refer, he gets 5% override on their referrals

### The "Friends" Program

For your other friends, use a simpler structure:
- **$100 cash per activated customer** + **20% of base for 6 months**
- No ongoing after 6 months (these are casual referrers, not salespeople)
- On Growth: $100 + $60/mo × 6 = **$460 per customer**

**Your friends don't need to sell.** They just need to say "my friend built this cool AI thing for businesses" at a dinner party or in a group chat. The $100 cash makes them remember to mention it.

## 4. Channel 3: Campus Ambassadors

### Why This Works

College students are:
- **Cheap** (they'll work for $15-20/hour equivalent or commission)
- **Hungry** (they want resume experience AND money)
- **Everywhere** (every city has a university near small businesses)
- **Social** (they'll post about it on LinkedIn/TikTok/Instagram)
- **Walking into businesses daily** (haircuts, car repairs, restaurants)

### The Campus Ambassador Program

**Structure:**

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Title** | "CallSaver Campus Ambassador" |
| **Commitment** | 5-10 hours/week |
| **Commission** | $150 per activated customer |
| **Bonus** | $500 bonus for first 5 customers, extra $1,000 for 10 in a semester |
| **Resume value** | "Business Development Representative" title for LinkedIn |
| **Tools provided** | Demo account, sales script, flyers, business cards |

**What they do:**
1. Walk into local businesses (salons, auto shops, restaurants, law offices) and ask "Do you ever miss phone calls?"
2. Get the owner's contact info
3. Send a follow-up email with a personalized demo link
4. You close the sale on a video call (they set the meeting, you do the demo)

**This is NOT cold calling.** This is feet-on-the-ground local business development. It works because:
- Business owners respect in-person visits more than cold calls/emails
- A friendly college student asking a question is non-threatening
- The student isn't selling — they're qualifying and getting you warm intros

### Scale

- 1 ambassador at 1 university doing 10 business visits/week
- 10% conversion to meeting = 1 meeting/week
- 50% meeting-to-close = 2 customers/month per ambassador
- 10 ambassadors across 10 cities = **20 customers/month**

**Cost:** 20 customers × $150 = $3,000/month. Revenue: 20 × $700 = $14,000/month. **4.7x ROI from day 1.**

### How to Recruit Ambassadors

1. **LinkedIn posts** — "Hiring campus ambassadors at [University]. $150 per customer. Flexible hours. Huge resume builder."
2. **University job boards** — Handshake, college career centers
3. **Business/entrepreneurship clubs** — these kids are EXACTLY who you want
4. **Bulletin boards** — yes, physical bulletin boards in business school buildings still work
5. **Greek life** — fraternities and sororities are networks. Get one member, they bring friends.
6. **r/sidehustle, r/beermoney** — Reddit communities for people looking to earn

**Start with 2-3 ambassadors at universities in cities with strong trade/service economies** (Phoenix, Dallas, Atlanta, Denver, Charlotte). Don't spread too thin.

## 6. Channel 5: LLM SEO & AI Search Optimization

### What Is LLM SEO?

When people ask ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overview "what's the best AI receptionist for plumbers?", you want to be in the answer.

### How LLM SEO Works

LLMs pull from:
1. **Web crawl data** — your website, blog posts, landing pages
2. **Structured data** — Schema.org markup, FAQ pages
3. **Third-party mentions** — review sites, comparison articles, directories
4. **Wikipedia/authoritative sources** — if you're mentioned there

### Tactics (Most Are Free)

1. **Create comparison pages** — "CallSaver vs Smith.ai vs Ruby" with structured data. LLMs LOVE comparison pages.

2. **Get listed on directories:**
   - G2.com (free listing)
   - Capterra (free listing, paid for premium placement)
   - GetApp (free listing)
   - Product Hunt (free launch)
   - AlternativeTo.net (free)
   - SaaSWorthy (free)

3. **FAQ schema on every page.** LLMs disproportionately pull from FAQ structured data:
   ```json
   {
     "@type": "FAQPage",
     "mainEntity": [{
       "@type": "Question",
       "name": "What is the best AI receptionist for plumbers?",
       "acceptedAnswer": {
         "@type": "Answer",
         "text": "CallSaver is the top-rated AI receptionist for plumbers..."
       }
     }]
   }
   ```

4. **"Best [X] for [Y]" blog posts** — "Best AI Answering Service for Contractors (2026 Guide)" with a comparison table. These get cited by LLMs constantly.

5. **Get mentioned in existing roundup articles.** Search for "best AI receptionist" and reach out to the authors asking to be included. Many bloggers accept free accounts in exchange for inclusion.

### Paid LLM SEO (When You Have Budget)

- **Sponsored articles on industry blogs** — $200-500/article. Gets you a backlink AND LLM training data mention.
- **Capterra/G2 premium placement** — $500-1,500/month. Very high intent traffic.
- **"Best of" list sponsorship** — Pay to be featured in "Top 10 AI Tools for Small Business" articles on Medium, Forbes contributor networks, etc. $300-1,000 per placement.

**Can it be gamed? Somewhat.** Creating lots of comparison content, getting directory listings, and building backlinks from relevant sites does influence LLM outputs. It's not quite "gaming" — it's just good SEO that also happens to train LLMs. The businesses doing this now are getting a massive head start.

| **Macro** (200K-1M) | General audience | $5,000-20,000 | $10,000-50,000 | Usually not worth it for B2B |

### The Right Influencers for CallSaver

**Don't target big tech influencers.** Target TRADE influencers:

- **YouTube:** "Electrician U" (1.2M subs), "HVAC School" (200K), "Roger Wakefield" (1.3M plumbing)
- **TikTok:** Trade content creators showing job sites, business tips
- **Instagram:** Local contractor influencers in target cities
- **LinkedIn:** Small business consultants, SCORE mentors, SBA advisors

### Recommended Approach (Bootstrapped)

1. **Affiliate, not sponsorship.** Don't pay upfront. Offer 20-25% recurring commission. They only get paid when their audience converts. Cost to you: $0 upfront.

2. **Send them a free account.** Let them use it for their own business first. When they love it, they'll talk about it authentically.

3. **Create "Partner Program" landing pages.** `callsaver.ai/partners/electrician-u` — custom landing page per influencer with their branding. Makes them feel special.

4. **Target nano/micro influencers in trades.** 20 nano influencers at $100/video = $2,000 for 20 videos across YouTube/TikTok/Instagram. Better ROI than 1 macro influencer at $10,000.

**Budget recommendation:** $0 for Month 1-2 (use affiliate only). $500-1,000/month starting Month 3 for nano influencer videos.

## 10. The Travis Playbook — Your First Force Multiplier

Here's the exact step-by-step for your first 2 weeks:

### Week 1: Activate Travis

**Day 1-2:**
- Call Travis. Not text, CALL. Have a 30-minute conversation.
- Explain what you built. Show him a live demo (call the demo number together).
- Make the offer: $200 cash per sign-up + 40% of base subscription for 12 months.
- Ask: "Who are the first 5 guys you'd call? Let's make a list right now."

**Day 3-4:**
- Travis texts/calls his 5 contacts: "Hey man, my friend built this crazy AI thing that answers your phone and books jobs. It's legit. Can I have him call you for 5 minutes?"
- You schedule 15-minute video demos with each one.

**Day 5-7:**
- Run the demos. Show the product. Let them call the demo line live.
- Close them: "10-day free trial, no credit card, cancel anytime. Let's get you set up right now."
- Goal: 2-3 sign-ups from Travis's first 5 intros.

### Week 2: Expand

- Pay Travis his $200-600 immediately. He needs to feel the money is REAL.
- Ask Travis: "Who else do you know? What about guys at your supply house?"
- Ask Travis: "Would any of your buddies want to make money referring people too?"
- Set up Travis with a simple referral link: `callsaver.ai/ref/travis`

**Travis should be worth 8-15 customers in the first 3 months.** At $200 each + $120/mo commissions, he'll be making $2,400-4,400+ in that period. He'll be VERY motivated.

### Expanding the Trade Network

Once Travis is producing:
1. Ask his first customers for referrals too ("Do you know anyone else who misses calls?")
2. Ask Travis to bring a friend to a local contractor meetup where you present
3. Offer Travis's friends the same deal (30% base for 12 months instead of 40%)
4. Each new referrer creates a chain: Travis → his electrician friends → THEIR plumber/HVAC friends

## 11. Commission & Revenue Share Structures

### Summary of All Programs

| Program | Who | Upfront | Recurring | Duration | Usage Share |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Travis (founding partner)** | Travis specifically | $200/customer | 40% of base | 12 months, then 10% | 0% |
| **Trade referrers** | Blue collar friends of Travis | $100/customer | 30% of base | 12 months, then 10% | 0% |
| **Friends & family** | Your personal network | $100/customer | 20% of base | 6 months, then 0% | 0% |
| **Agency reseller** | Marketing agencies | Wholesale pricing | Keep their markup | Ongoing | 20% of usage |
| **Agency referral** | Marketing agencies (simple) | $0 | 25% of base | 12 months, then 10% | 0% |
| **Campus ambassador** | College students | $150/customer | 0% | One-time | 0% |
| **Influencer affiliate** | Content creators | $0 | 20% of base | 12 months | 0% |

### Why Usage Revenue Is Always Yours

Usage ($1.00-1.15/min) is where 50-70% of your revenue comes from. **Never share usage revenue with referral partners** (except the agency reseller model where they're doing ongoing account management). Here's why:

1. Usage revenue covers your variable costs (LiveKit, LLM tokens, telephony)
2. Usage revenue is unpredictable — sharing it makes your economics unstable
3. Partners don't influence usage (the customer's call volume is independent of the referrer)
4. Base subscription commission is enough to motivate — $897-1,440/year per customer is very generous

### Tracking & Payouts

Use **Stripe Connect** or **Rewardful** or **PartnerStack** for automated tracking:
- Each partner gets a unique referral code/link
- Customer signs up → attributed to partner forever
- Monthly payout via ACH or PayPal
- Dashboard showing referrals, revenue, commissions

**For Month 1, do this manually in a spreadsheet.** Don't build partner infrastructure before you have partners. Track in Notion or Google Sheets, pay via Venmo/Zelle. Automate when you hit 10+ active partners.

## 12. Customer Support & Success Strategy

### 12.1 Your Instinct Is Right: Human Sales, AI Support

```
SALES:      Human-first (YOU, then trained reps)     ← Trust matters
ONBOARDING: Human-guided (YOU or support, with docs) ← Complexity matters  
SUPPORT:    AI-first, human escalation               ← Speed matters
SUCCESS:    Automated + human check-ins               ← Scale matters
```

**Outbound AI voice agents for cold sales = BAD IDEA.** You're right. Business owners get robocalled 10x/day. An AI cold call would instantly destroy credibility. The irony of an AI call company using AI to cold call is not lost on anyone.

**Inbound support via AI = GREAT IDEA.** When a customer has a question at 11 PM, they want an answer NOW. Intercom's AI agent (Fin) can handle 60-80% of support queries without human intervention.

### 12.2 Intercom Setup — Support Tiers

You already have Intercom in the frontend. Here's how to structure it:

**Tier 1: Intercom Fin (AI Agent) — 24/7, instant**
- Answer FAQs: "How do I change my agent's greeting?"
- Guide through settings: "Go to Settings → Agent → Edit greeting"
- Provide status: "Your agent handled 12 calls today"
- Troubleshoot common issues: "Your Jobber connection shows as healthy"
- Link to help docs

**Tier 2: Human Support (You, initially) — business hours, <2 hour response**
- Complex integration issues
- Billing questions
- Bug reports
- Anything Fin can't answer → escalated automatically

**Tier 3: Emergency (phone/text) — for outages only**
- "My phone number isn't working" → immediate response
- This should be rare if your infrastructure is solid

### 12.3 Intercom Fin Setup Checklist

1. **Feed it your help docs** — write 20-30 help articles covering:
   - Getting started / onboarding
   - Connecting Jobber / HCP / GCal
   - Customizing the voice agent
   - Understanding billing / usage
   - Troubleshooting common issues
   - How to read call transcripts
   - How to manage the service catalog

2. **Set conversation routing rules:**
   - Billing/cancellation → immediate human escalation
   - "Agent not working" / outage → immediate human escalation
   - Everything else → Fin tries first, escalates after 2 failed attempts

3. **Enable proactive messages:**
   - Day 1 of trial: "Welcome! Here's how to set up your agent in 5 minutes"
   - Day 3: "Have you connected your CRM? Here's how →"
   - Day 7: "Your agent has handled X calls! Here's your weekly summary"
   - Day 9: "Your trial ends tomorrow. Questions? We're here to help."

### 12.4 Customer Success — Automated + Personal

**Automated (everyone):**
- Weekly email digest: calls handled, appointments booked, estimated revenue
- Monthly ROI report: total value delivered vs. cost
- Feature announcements: "New! Review management is now available"
- Usage alerts: "Your agent handled 50% more calls this week — great sign!"

**Personal (high-value or at-risk):**
- **Onboarding call** (15 min) for every new customer — YOU do this for the first 50 customers. It builds trust, gets feedback, and reduces churn by 40-60%.
- **Monthly check-in** for Enterprise customers
- **Rescue call** when a customer hasn't logged in for 14 days or shows signs of churning (no calls, no logins, no integration activity)

### 12.5 Support as a Sales Tool

Every support interaction is a chance to upsell:

```
Customer: "Can CallSaver respond to my Google reviews?"
Fin:      "We're launching Google Business Profile management soon! 
           I can add you to the waitlist and notify you when it's available.
           In the meantime, here's how to set up review notifications..."
```

```
Customer: "My website is slow and I'm not getting enough Google traffic"
Fin:      "We offer website health audits! I can run a free PageSpeed 
           analysis for you right now. Would you like that?"
```

### 12.6 Where AI Agents DO Work for Sales/Marketing

While outbound AI cold calls are bad, there ARE good AI-for-marketing plays:

1. **Intercom Fin on your marketing website** — visitors ask "how does it work?" and Fin answers instantly, qualifies them, and books a demo call with you. This is inbound, not outbound. Huge difference.

2. **AI-generated follow-up emails** — after a demo, AI drafts a personalized follow-up email that you review and send. Saves 10 min per lead.

3. **AI-powered lead scoring** — analyze which trial users are most likely to convert based on usage patterns (calls handled, integrations connected, login frequency). Focus your personal time on the highest-potential leads.

4. **AI chat on your landing page** — "Chat with our AI to see how CallSaver would work for YOUR business." Interactive, engaging, zero human cost.

5. **AI-generated content** — blog posts, social media posts, email campaigns. You edit for voice/accuracy, but AI does 80% of the writing.
